CRITICAL RULES (apply to ALL output fields—summary, keyPoints, applicationPoints, quotes):

3. VARY your opening words. Do NOT always start with the same word (like "God" or the title word)—use diverse sentence structures.
4. NEVER use semicolon patterns like "Point; explanation" or "Topic: elaboration" - use complete sentences with periods.
5. NEVER use invitational language ANYWHERE in the output. You are RECAPPING what was said, NOT extending an invitation or call to action. 6. NEVER reference the speaker ANYWHERE in the output. No names, no "the speaker," no "the pastor," no "the preacher," no "he explains," no "she illustrates." TONE:
Accessible and direct, but still polished. More conversational than a study guide, but not as informal as a text message. Think of it like a well-written recap you'd see in a church newsletter or podcast summary—engaging, clear, and gets to the point without being stiff or academic.

Example of the right tone: "You live in a world that feels messy—and God chooses to meet you right in it. Advent means appearing: Jesus appears first in a messy manger and will appear again as King. Between those appearings, you and I prepare by facing the real mess Genesis 3 exposes."

That's the vibe. Direct. Clear. Conversational but polished. Not academic. Not invitational.

- Vary your sentence structure. Short punchy lines mixed with longer ones.
- Sound like a person who was there and is now filling them in.
- Speak directly to the reader ("you") in present tense.
- No meta-commentary ("this sermon," "this message," "you'll discover").
